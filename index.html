About A. P. J. Abdul Kalam


Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam (Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam) was an Indian aerospace scientist and statesman who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Known as the "Missile Man of India" for his work on the development of ballistic missile technology,he played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development.


    Early Life and Education


  • Born: October 15,1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.

  • Family Background: His father, Jainulabdeen, was a boat owner and imam of a local mosque; his mother, Ashiamma, was a housewife.

  • Education: He completed his schooling at Schwartz Higher Secondary School in Ramanathapuram and later graduated in physics from Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli. He pursued aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT), Chennai.


    • Career in Science and Technology


  • Early Career: Kalam joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) as a scientist after graduating from MIT. He also worked for the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO).

      • Key Contributions:


    • SLV-III Project: He was the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III), which deployed the Rohini satellite in near-Earth orbit in 1980.

    • Missile Development: Played a leading role in developing the Agni and Prithvi missiles, earning him the nickname "Missile Man of India."

    • Pokhran-II Tests:He was a key figure in India's nuclear weapons tests in 1998.


    Presidency


  • Term:2002-2007

      • Highlights:


    • Known for his inspirational speeches and efforts to engage with the youth.

    • Advocated for education and innovation, urging young Indians to dream big.

    • Worked to transform the office of the President into a platform for advancing scientific and technological development.


    Post-Presidency and Legacy


  • Post-Presidential Work:After his term, Kalam became a visiting professor at various institutions and continued to promote education and technology.

  • Books and Writings: Authored several books including "Wings of Fire,""Wings of Fire," an autobiography; "Ignited Minds"; and "India 2020."

  • Death: Passed away onJuly 27,2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong.


    • Honors and Awards


  • Bharat Ratna:India's highest civilian award, among many others.

  • International Recognition:Acknowledged globally for his contributions to science and technology.
